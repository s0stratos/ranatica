General rules
-----------------------------------------------

1/ division into syllables within words

1a) each vowel forms the core (nucleus) of a distinct syllable

fī.li.um (LsL)

1b) the latin diphtongs or vowel digraphs count as a single syllable core (nucleus)

eu.nū.cum (LLL)
plau.tī (LL)
cē.te.rae (LsL)

1c) when a single consonant separate vowels it is grouped with the following vowel.

fī.li.um (LsL) not fīl.i.um 
eu.nū.cum (LLL) not eun.ū.cum not eu.nūc.um not eun.ūc.um
cē.te.rae (LsL) not cēt.e.rae not cē.ter.ae not not cēt.er.ae

1d) when two consonants separate vowels they get split between the syllables

pec.cā.tum (LLL) not pe.ccā.tum not pecc.ā.tum
per.sō.nīs (LLL) not pe.rsō.nīs
nes.ci.ō (LsL) not ne.sci.ō

1e) the following groups involving r and l don't get split unless they are on a word-internal boundary

br/bl
cr/cl
dr/dl
fr/fl
gr/gl
pr/pl
tr/tl

pū.bli.cum (LsL) not pūb.li.cum
ob.lec.tā (LLL) not o.blec.tā
sub.li.nē.tur (LsLL) not su.bli.nē.tur
neg.le.ge.re (Lsss) not ne.gle.ge.re
dē.glup.ta (LLs) not dēg.lup.ta

1f) when three or four consonants separate vowels the first one groups with the previous syllable

ter.res.tris (LLL) not ter.re.stris
dīx.stī (LL) (= dīc.stī)
per.scrī.ban.tur (LLLL)
per.stre.punt (LsL) 

1f) in some cases the group is simplified in words with prefixes

pos.(t)prīn.ci.pi.a (LLsss) 
pos.(t)quam (LL)

1g) any consonants before the first vowel group with it.

prīn.ci.pi.um (LccL)
sci.unt (LL)

2/ division into syllables within words

2a) the start of words cannot be resyllabified

e.go . spon.de.ō not e.go s.pon.de.ō

2b) on the other hand the end of words is systematically resyllabified

pa.te.r ē.gre.di.tur (ssLssL) not pa.ter . ē.gre.di.tur

2/ short and long syllables

syllables count as short if they have a short vowel as a core and no consonant follows the core.

otherwise it's long.

3/ elision 

when two vowels clash into each other in latin between two words or between a preposition and the word, they form a new vowel group. 
In Terentius metrically it manifests the following way. 

either the results amounts to ignoring the first vowel.

ita aiiunt => i.t(a) ai.iunt 

or the results amounts to considering the first vowel is shortened.

dē+amō => de.a.mō

the two vowels can NEVER coexist without clashing.

what happens at breaks triggering end of line conditions
---------------------------------------------------------

end of line conditions favor the recognition of the end of line patterns Lsx|||| and xLx||||

Lsx||||

elements favoring L > final s pronounced 
elements favoring sX > strict breaking of function words into iambs (mihi, suom, tuom..), use of iambic forms like ier infinitives
elements favoring X as L > "brevis in longo"
